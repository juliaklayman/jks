JULIA KLAYMAN

juliaklayman@gmail.com

My name is Julia Klayman, and I am a student at George W. Hewlett High School. I have always loved to volunteer with children, which sparked my interest in special education. I am the president of the Friendship Circle Organization, where I volunteer weekly with special needs children. I also love research, which has lead me to participate in the National History Day Competition yearly, where I have made it to the national competition twice. I am also enrolled in the science research course at my school, and I have been researching projects related to UV light since sophomore year.

Research

I have been taking science research since 9th grade and it has always been one of my favorite classes. In my sophomore year, I worked on a project about UV light, aiming to identify and characterize the photoreceptor, UVR8, in a plant called Ceratopteris richardii. Building off the results from this project, in my junior year, I worked on a project determining the cytotoxic effect of Titanium Dioxide particle size and UV light on a different plant, Lemna minor. This course has sparked my interest in research, and I know that in the future I would love to continue working on these projects.

Makeup

I have always loved makeup. I watched my mother when I was younger, and fell in love with the art of makeup. I began to watch Youtube videos, and learn special techniques to get “the perfect look.” I started off with neutral tones, and as I got better, I moved on to brighter colors. When I get stressed, doing makeup is my creative outlet. Now, I do all of my friends and family’s makeup for any occasion, makeup for proms and other events, and makeup on myself just for fun.

BBYO

BBYO is a non-profit organization that helps teach teens about their Jewish identity and provides incredible leadership programs. When I joined BBYO as a general member in 9th grade, I didn’t know anyone in the club. I ended up falling in love with BBYO and the people I met at meetings and conventions. I became more and more involved and in 10th grade, I ran for treasurer and won. Currently, I am the vice president on board and plan to run for president next year.

National History Day

In 8th grade, the National History Day Fair became a requirement to take advanced social studies courses in the high school. In 9th grade, unlike a majority of the grade, I continued with history fair and it has become one of my favorite projects that I do every year. With great success in 8th grade, making it to the national competition, I was confident we would make it to nationals again! In 10th grade, my group and I won second place at the county and the state level, moving onto nationals. It was an amazing experience to meet the other local and international students, and to present our hard work to the judges!

Friendship Circle

In middle school, I was involved in Bulldog Buddies, a club where you go and spend time with the students with special needs before and after school. I loved it so much, so when I was about to start high school, I knew I wanted to get more involved. I found the local chapter of the Friendship Circle organization, and I knew it was the perfect volunteer opportunity for me. I was matched with a family, and weekly, I go to their house to do homework with both of the children. I work with both the son, who has special needs and the daughter who does not. I love working with them, so I became more involved in the organization. I help with the summer and winter camps held for any child with special needs, where we take field trips to engage the children in activities that they wouldn’t normally get to do. I have been the student president since 9th grade, and help match volunteers with special friends just like mine.

Thailand

In the summer of 2019, I took a service trip to Thailand. When I was in Thailand, I learned about Southeast Asian culture and took part in service projects. My favorite project was going to schools that did not gain enough government funding and helped teach the students English. We were able to teach them how to tell the time in English, how to say animals and common phrases. While we were there, they also taught us some Thai. It was an incredible experience. We also volunteered at an elephant sanctuary. We learned how to clean, bathe, and care for the elephants, and learn about their journey to be released back into the wild.
